Nikola Tesla

"The visionary who electrified the world and inspired the future."

Nikola Tesla was a brilliant inventor, engineer, and futurist who revolutionized the way we use and distribute electricity. Know for his work with alternating current (AC) systems, wireless energy, and innovations in electromagnetism, Tesla's ideas laid the foundation for modern technology. His life was a testament to genius, creativity, and relentless determination.

Here is a timeline of Nikola Tesla's life

Early Life

  • Born: July 10, 1856 in Smijan, Croatia
  • Education: Studied engineering and physics at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz and later at the Charles-Ferdinand University in Prague.

Major Contributions

  1. Alternating Current(AC):
    • Tesla's AC system became the standard for electrical power distribution worldwide.
  2. Tesla Coil:
    • Invented the Tesla Coil in 1891, a key component in wireless communication and radio technology.
  3. Wireless Power:
    • Proposed and experimented with wireless energy transfer, including the ambitious Wardenclyffe Tower Project.
  4. Innovative Motors:
    • Developed the first practical induction motor, used widely in industrial applications.

Legacy

  • The SI unit of magnetic flux density, known as Tesla, is named after him.
  • His contributions paved the way for modern radio, television, and even smartphones.
  • Despite financial struggles, his visionary ideas continue to inspire scientists and engineers worldwide today.

Interesting Facts

  • Tesla claimed he could envision detailed inventions in his mind without drawing them.
  • He once lit 200 lamps wirelessly from 25 miles away during his experiments in Colorado Springs
  • Tesla's rivalry with Thomas Edison over AC vs. DC became known as the "War of the Currents".
